## **CONCEPT PROPOSAL STRUCTURE AND PITCH PROTOCOL** 

The Concept Proposal shall be evaluated according to the following time allocation and structure: 

Each section will be given **one (1) hour and thirty (30) minutes** to present their concepts. There should be one (1) lead to pitch the three-concept overview, and one (1) pitcher per module. 

**Pitching for overall three concepts:** 3 minutes **Pitching per module:** (2 minutes per module x 10 modules) x 3 concepts = 60 minutes 

- **Q&A Session:** 20 minutes 

- **Deliberation:** 7 Minutes 

## **Pitch Deck for the Overall Concepts:** 

- **1.Problem:** Identification of the organizational or societal 

- gap. 

**2.Proposed Solution:** A detailed explanation of how the system works and the process used to address the stated problems. 

## **Pitch Deck per Module:** 

1. **System Module:** Describe the core business logic and functional purpose of this component. 

2. **Process Flow:** Visualizing the UI/UX design. 

3. **ERD:** Mapping the relational database structure. 4. **Network Diagram:** Illustrating the logical and physical network layout. 

_**Note:** No scripts allowed. Anyone reading from a script will be immediately interrupted and asked to stop._

**TEAM COMPOSITION AND ROLES** Each team shall consist of a maximum of **four (4) members** with clearly defined responsibilities: 

- **Project Lead:** Responsible for project management, budget, and work plan execution. **Systems Analyst:** Ensures technical coordination and integration across all system modules. **Programmer:** Handles the design, coding, and testing of software components. **Technical Documenter:** Responsible for the manuscript and technical system documentation. 

## **CONCEPT PRESENTATION STANDARDS** 

- **I. Professional and Behavioral Standards Dress Code:** Corporate Business Attire is strictly required. Prohibited items include jeans, rubber shoes, polo shirts, t-shirts, slippers, and crop tops. All _**See attached**_ 

- members must be well-groomed. ( _**sample**_ ) **Attendance:** Attendance is **mandatory** for all members during the oral presentation. Failure to attend without a valid excuse results in an automatic **FAILED** remark for the course.

## **II. Required Technical Content** 

- Proponents must present the following to the panel: **Systems Integration (IT 224):** A BPMN diagram mapping the integrated workflow. **Web Systems (IT 223):** UI/UX presentation of the proposed module. (May use figma or canva) **Networking 1 (IT 222):** A network diagram with complete IP address and labels. **Information** An **Management (IT 221):** Entity-Relationship 

- Diagram (ERD) per module. 

## **III. Documentation Requirements** 

- The presentation must be supported by a concept paper following the given template, and provide one (1) hardcopy for each concept: **Title:** Must provide a comprehensive picture of the study and mention the technology used to improve it. **Background of the Study:** A narrative of how the study was 

   - formulated with properly acknowledged citations. 

- Presented using a deductive method, moving from general knowledge to specific details. 

- Must contrast existing technology with your proposed technology and its advantages. 

   - **Statement of the Problem:** A clear statement of the specific problems your study aims to address. **Objectives:** 

- _General Objective:_ A single sentence summarizing the main 

- goal (e.g., "To develop a unified platform for..."). _Specific Objectives:_ 3-4 bullet points using technical action 

- verbs such as design, automate, integrate, and secure. 

   - **Proposed Solution:** A detailed explanation of how the system works and the process used to address the stated problems. **System Modules:** A list of the 10 major components of your software, detailing the functions or features of each. **BPMN Diagram:** Mapping of the integrated workflow.
